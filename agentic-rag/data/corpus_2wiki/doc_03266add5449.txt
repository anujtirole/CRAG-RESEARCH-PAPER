Roman I of Kiev

Roman Rostislavich ( Cyrillic: Роман Ростиславич)( died 1180), Prince of Smolensk( 1160 – 1172, 1177 – 1180), Grand Prince of Kiev( 1171 – 1173, 1175 – 1177) and Prince of Novgorod( 1178 – 1179). He was the son of Rostislav Mstislavich. Temporarily installed as Grand Prince of Kiev in July 1171, he was quickly replaced by Andrey Bogolyubsky's brother, Mikhail of Vladimir. He had a son: Mstislav III of Kiev.
